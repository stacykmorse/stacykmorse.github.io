Software Training eLearning Course

I created this interactive, video-based elearning course to demonstrate upcoming interface updates for an applicant tracking software. "Meal and Rest Breaks" eLearning Course

This course was created for a large healthcare company with employees located throughout the United States. The company needed a solution to ensure all employees were aware of their rights and responsibilities related to meal and rest breaks. This was complicated by the fact that labor laws vary from state to state.

To address this challenge, I created a learning solution focused on Federal regulations, such as the Fair Labor Standards Act, as well as company policy. After presenting the information, I included interactive exercises to encourage the learner to apply what they'd just learned in order to increase retention. In addition, I created an interactive map designed to allow employees to explore the labor laws in their home state.

The template I created for this course was also adopted for several subsequent courses developed for the same organization. The course was built in Articulate Storyline 2 with custom graphics created in Adobe Illustrator.

  • Created For: Large healthcare company
  • Date: December 2016
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline 2, Adobe Illustrator CC

"Information Security Awareness" eLearning Course

I designed, developed, and narrated this elearning course for a Fortune 500 healthcare company. The course is part of a mandatory annual education series, which is required for all of the organization's approximately 40,000 employees. The course involves several interactive activities designed to have learners engage with the content and practice the desired behaviors, such as keeping company assets secure, reporting and deleting suspicious emails, encrypting emails with confidential information, and creating strong passwords. Learners are also asked to match relevant regulations to corresponding examples, identify consequences for noncompliance, and explore best practices for protecting both company and personal information.

The course was built in Articulate Storyline, but I also incorporated custom JavaScript to achieve the functionality of several of the more complex activities. It was also important to ensure that the course looked and functioned consistently across multiple environments, due to the large and diverse audience for the course. I worked to ensure that all activities and interactive elements of the course displayed and functioned properly on numerous devices (including computers, tablets, and smartphones) and in numerous environments (including older browsers such as IE 7 and 8).

  • Created For: Fortune 500 healthcare company
  • Date: November 2015
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline 2, Adobe Illustrator CC, Adobe Audition CC, custom JavaScript

Electronic Documentation eLearning Course

In the fall of 2014, Advance Financial began to transition from paper loan documentation to electronic loan documentation. Tablets were installed in the branch locations, which customers use to electronically sign contracts and other loan-related documents. This new process represented a significant change for the Customer Service Representatives, who are responsible for completing all loan transactions with customers.

This course was created to instruct the CSRs on the technical aspect of the new process, while also reinforcing the company’s Customer Experience standards, which are an integral part of the CSRs’ responsibilities. These standards include the specific language CSRs use while communicating with customers, as well other customer service expectations.

The course features software simulations of the system CSRs use to process loans, as well as simulated customer interactions in which learners must choose the best response to the customer.

  • Created For: Advance Financial
  • Date: September 2014
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline 1, Adobe Photoshop CC

"Money Order Log" eLearning Course

Created for Advance Financial, this elearning course was designed to improve the completion rate and accuracy of Money Order Log entries by the company’s Customer Service Representatives. I used characters and narrative techniques to contextualize the information, and interactive simulations to illicit performance from learners. This allowed them to actively practice the desired behaviors and get feedback based on their actions.

This course required extensive use of Adobe Photoshop to create course elements. This included altering stock photos (by changing clothing colors and adding company logos and nametags) to ensure that the “employees” shown in the course accurately reflected the company uniform. It also involved the creation of numerous sample customer identification documents, such as driver’s licenses from multiple states, a Permanent Resident card, and Social Security cards.

Note: This demo requires Adobe Flash Player.

  • Created For: Advance Financial
  • Date: October 2014
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline 2, Adobe Photoshop CC, Adobe Illustrator CC

"The Four Styles of Leadership" eLearning Course

As part of a new soft skills curriculum launched by Advance Financial in mid-2014, I was tasked with creating several courses on leadership. This elearning course is based on a simple four-style model of leadership styles that was designed to be easy for our employees to understand and apply. The objectives of this course were for employees to be able to identify each of the four styles, recognize the pros and cons of each style and when it is most effective, and then be able to select the most effective leadership approach to apply in a given situation.

To illustrate the uses (and misuses) of each style, I used an engaging comic book style to tell stories. I used characters to personify each style to aid in retention of knowledge, and I used challenging scenarios to get learners to think about the content in context. The course also includes a self-assessment in which learners find out which style is closest to their natural tendencies, and to discover which styles they may need to work on.

  • Created For: Advance Financial
  • Date: April 2014
  • Tools Used: Articulate Storyline 1, Adobe Illustrator CC, custom JavaScript
